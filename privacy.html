About this Policy

This policy explains when and why we collect personal data about you, how we use it, how we keep it secure, and your rights and obligations in relation to it. It applies to full members, temporary members, applicants for membership, former members whose membership has ceased and guests who also attend our events.

In order to access the website and to participate in the events we organise, we ask you to provide certain information by which you can be identified. You can be assured that it will only be used in accordance with this Data Privacy Policy.

Royal Naval Volunteer Reserve Yacht Club (the Club) is committed to complying with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) when dealing with your personal data. How the GDPR applies to you is explained at ico.org.uk/your-data-matters.

We keep this Policy under regular review, and may change it from time to time, so please check this page for the latest version. Please also refer to the Club Rules, Section 24: Data and Data Protection.

When do we collect your personal data?

When you join the Club, you complete an application form. The information you provide, together (since 2020) with any letter of recommendation from an existing member submitted in support of your application, is stored on the website and forms the basis of your member profile.

In addition:

  • you will be asked to provide your bank and contact details to our third party subscription collection company, GoCardless;
  • if you pay for Slops items or register and pay for events online, the details entered are stored on the website and by a third party payment processing entity, Stripe; and
  • photographs and videos by which you may be identified may be taken at Club events which you attend, for subsequent Club use.

Compliance with this Data Privacy Policy

Club Rule 4(b) provides that, upon election to membership, each newly elected member shall be taken to have agreed to comply with the Rules. By Rule 24 (b) each member undertakes to read and abide by the Club's Data Privacy Policy, in its latest version, as published on the Club's website.

What do we collect?

Personal data about you that is stored in your member profile will include, but is not limited to, the following:

  • Name, address and contact details
  • Photo, if supplied
  • Service history and current status
  • Boating experience and qualifications
  • Boat and permit details
  • Letter of recommendation by your proposer, if applicable

When you register for an event, a record of your attendance at the event is kept on the website, together with any invoice issued to you in respect of any requisite payment. An invoice is also issued and retained on the website for any items you purchase directly from Slops.

Further financial information is collected and retained by the IT systems of Stripe, GoCardless and banks. Photographs and video submitted for use by the Club may be copied into our photo repository, Flickr, retained in our archives, used on the website or included in newsletters. Videos of Zoom meetings may be retained in Vimeo for subsequent viewing, including AGMs conducted by Zoom.

Why do we collect personal data?

We require your personal data to run the Club for the benefit of our members in accordance with the Club's Objects as set out in our Club Rules.

The following is a list of some of the purposes for which your personal data is used:

  • Management of your membership.
  • Provision of an online membership directory, for members to contact each other and gain information about another member's background and boating experience.
  • Provision of a Handbook, which includes members' contact details and information on members' boats, Club permits and roles held within the Club.
  • To inform you of Club events and news by means of e-bulletins using your email address.
  • To send newsletters and the Handbook by post to your home address.
  • For the Committee to use for its legitimate purposes, including research into historic event attendances or membership demographics.
  • For Event Organisers to contact you and provide your details to venues and marinas as necessary for running events.
  • To enable relevant people to view invoices and obtain information about their payment.
  • For you to produce evidence of Club membership to affiliated clubs.

Who has access?

Club administrators

To enable management of the Club's affairs, the Committee has authorised the following Officers and post-holders to access personal data on the website in order to fulfil their roles:

  • Commodore
  • Hon. Treasurer
  • Hon. Secretary
  • Membership Secretary
  • Website Manager
  • Data Protection Officer
  • Warrant Secretary

Any one or more of these persons may download members' personal data to their personal devices for the legitimate purpose of performing their roles. They are required to abide by the GDPR and this Data Privacy Policy.

Event Organisers

For the duration of a specific event, relevant Event Organisers are provided with access to the Events section of the website. They can view registration details, invoices and payment status. At the conclusion of the event their access is removed and they must delete any members' personal data downloaded to run the event.

Slops and other members

The Slops Bosun's additional access is limited to invoices and payment status for Slops orders. Members may download lists of registrants and participation details for an event in which they are participating, to enhance socialisation during the event. This data must be deleted once the event is over.

Financial providers

Limited payment information is visible to authorised people monitoring Stripe, GoCardless and the Club's banks. Stripe information is limited to the payer's name, card type, card issuer, expiry date and the final four digits. GoCardless access includes names and email addresses but not bank account details.

With which third parties and individuals do we share your personal data?

The Club will never sell your personal data.

We share the minimum personal data necessary with third parties for them to provide goods or services of benefit to you. We have no control over the Privacy Policies of our partner service providers.

Wild Apricot Inc

Wild Apricot provides the software for the Club website, membership and event management systems. Its staff should only access personal data when authorised by a Club administrator in response to a specific support request.

GoCardless and banks

GoCardless collects membership fees by Direct Debit. The Club shares your email address, while bank details entered for Direct Debit are held by GoCardless. Where an electronic bank transfer is required, authorised Club officers may need your bank account details.

Printers, venues and marinas

Names and postal addresses may be sent to printers for newsletters and handbooks, with deletion requested after dispatch. Venues and marinas may require attendee, boat, skipper and contact information to provide their services.

Affiliated Yacht Clubs

Names and contact details of Club event organisers may be shared with affiliated clubs when publicising our events.

Flickr and Vimeo

Flickr stores Club photographs and photo archives that may be publicly accessible. Vimeo stores historic Zoom recordings of some meetings and vTots, where displayed attendee names may remain visible.

RYA and Fasthosts

The Club shares anonymised data with the RYA to maintain affiliated status. Fasthosts provides domain management and email forwarding services.

How do we keep your personal data secure?

Website

The Club uses adequate and generally accepted standards of technology to protect personal data on the website by limiting access to members through individual passwords. Passwords used by members with administrative access are required to be very strong.

Financial systems

All financial systems, including those run by Stripe, GoCardless and the Club's banks, use strong, multi-factor authorisation.

Members

Members storing other members' personal data on their own devices are requested to use secure passwords and must delete data no longer required for the purpose for which it was obtained.

What rights do you have over your own personal data?

Under the GDPR you have the following rights:

  • to access your personal data;
  • to be provided with information about how your personal data is processed;
  • to have your personal data corrected;
  • to withdraw consent, object to or restrict processing, or have personal data erased in certain circumstances;
  • to withdraw consent for relevant details to appear in the Membership Directory and/or the next Club Handbook;
  • to have your personal data transferred to yourself or a business in certain circumstances;
  • to change within your member profile what information is visible to other members; and
  • to take complaints about how the Club processes your data to the Information Commissioner at ico.org.uk/concerns.

In all cases, please contact the DPO or the Membership Secretary for help.

What obligations do you have over other members' personal data?

You have the following obligations under the Club Rules and this Data Privacy Policy:

  • To ensure that your personal data held by the Club remains current, complete and accurate at all times.
  • To read and abide by the Club's current Data Privacy Policy.
  • Unless expressly permitted by the Committee or required for an administrative role, not to disclose members' personal data to non-members or use lists of members for personal purposes unrelated to the Club.
  • Not to use membership data for personal gain or commercial or marketing purposes.

Access to members' personal data is obtained by logging in to the website. You must maintain a very strong password and not share it. If you act for the Club in a role requiring members' data, you should delete that data when you no longer act in that capacity.

What happens to your personal data if your membership ceases?

If your membership ceases, your personal data will be archived at the beginning of the next Club year. Archived data is retained indefinitely for historical records, data analysis and potential legal claims. Your name may remain in event registrant lists, while other archived personal data is restricted to authorised Club website administrators. Your details will disappear from online Directories.

Under the GDPR, you may demand deletion of personal data from the database and archives. A paid-up member making such a demand will be deemed to have resigned. Deletion removes event attendance and completed transaction records, although financial systems retain invoice numbers and amounts without a link to the original paid invoices.

Historic photographs on Flickr and videos stored on Vimeo will not be deleted because they form part of the Club's archives. Members must not keep personal data relating to other members beyond the period necessary to fulfil an authorised Club role or task.

How does this policy apply to guests?

Non-member registrants may be sent contact details of event attendees where included in information provided by the Organiser before and during an event. Their own details may be included and they may be invited to join a WhatsApp group for the duration of the event.

Non-member registrants are covered by the Club's Combined Liability insurance policy. Records are retained securely for at least as long as necessary to meet tax obligations and maintain Club archives.

At the conclusion of the event, non-member registrants must delete contact details sent to them during the event, as the data then resumes its usual confidential status.

Any questions?

For questions about this Data Privacy Policy, please contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@rnvryc.org.

For help updating the data in your Profile, contact our Membership Secretary at membership@rnvryc.org.

Information on the GDPR can be found at ico.gov.uk.
